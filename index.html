Green Day

God's Favorite Band

From left to right: Mike Dirnt, Billie Joe Armstrong and Tré Cool


Timeline: From then to now

  • 1986 - It all starts in Oakland, California when the band Sweet Children came around. Founded by childhood friends Billie Joe (singing and playing guitar) and Mike Dirnt (backup vocals and bass guitar), the drummer John Kiffmeyer aka Al Sobrante joined them later the year later.
  • 1987 - Sweet Children's first live performance that took place on October 17 at Rod's Hickory Pit in Vallejo, California.
  • 1989 - The band changed its name to Green Day and released their debut EP 1,000 Hours on Lookout! Records.
  • 1990 - Their debut studio album 39/Smooth was released and recorded two EPs, Slappy and Sweet Children. Later this year, shortly after the band's first nationwide tour, Kiffmeyer left the East Bay area to attend University. The Lookouts drummer Tré Cool began filling in as a temporary replacement and later Cool's position as Green Day's drummer became permanent.
  • 1991 - Lookout! re-released 39/Smooth as 1,039/Smoothed Out Slappy Hours, now including songs from the band's first two EPs Slappy, and 1,000 Hours.
  • 1992 - The band's second studio album, Kerplunk, was released and sold 50,000 copies in the U.S.
  • 1993 - With Kerplunk's underground success, the band was approached with offers to sign with major labels. The band eventually left Lookout! and signed to Reprise Records.
  • 1994 - Breakthrough album Dookie was released in February and became a commercial success, helped by extensive MTV airplay for the videos of the songs "Longview", "Basket Case", and "When I Come Around", all of which reached the number one position on the Modern Rock Tracks charts.
  • 1995 - Dookie's follow up Insomniac came out in the Fall of this year, the songs were less poppy, faster and heavier than any of their previous work and, probably for this reason, not as commercially successful.
  • 1997 - Nimrod, an experimental deviation from the band's standard pop-punk brand of music, was released in October. "Good Riddance (Time of Your Life)" was the most different and successful single.
  • 1999 - Guitarist Jason White began supporting the band during concerts as rhythm guitarist.
  • 2000 - Green Day's sixth studio album Warning was released in October. There was a clear decline in the band's popularity, while previous albums had reached a status of at least double platinum, Warning was only certified gold. In the same year the live DVD Live Without Warning! was released.
  • 2001 - The compilation album (and DVD with music videos, plus 2 new songs) International Superhits! was released.
  • 2002 - A b-sides album Shenanigans was released. In the spring, Green Day co-headlined the Pop Disaster Tour with Blink-182, which was documented on the DVD Riding in Vans with Boys.
  • 2003 - In September the mysterious band The Network released an album called Money Money 2020, this band has nothing to do with Green Day though *wink wink*
  • 2004 - The punk rock opera album which follows the journey of the fictitious "Jesus of Suburbia" American Idiot debuted at number one on the Billboard charts, the band's first album to reach number one, it also won the 2005 Grammy for Best Rock Album.
  • 2005 - American Idiot won the 2005 Grammy for Best Rock Album and Green Day had the longest tour in their career yet. Releasing the epic live CD and DVD Bullet in a Bible filmed in Milton Keynes, UK. This was where Tré Cool jokingly attributed them the "God's Favorite Band" title.
  • 2007 - The group released an album under the name Foxboro Hot Tubs entitled Stop Drop and Roll!!! a 60's inspired garage rock n' roll album.
  • 2009 - The anticipated follow up to American Idiot, 21st Century Breakdown was released in May, the album reached number one in fourteen countries, being certified gold or platinum in each. 21st Century Breakdown achieved Green Day's best chart performance to date.
  • 2010 - American Idiot the Musical production, directed by award-winning director Michael Mayer, opened on Broadway and Green Day released the soundtrack to the musical. The show featured an expanded story of the original album, with new characters such as Will, Extraordinary Girl, and Favorite Son
  • 2011 - In March the band released another CD/DVD entitled Awesome as F**k.
  • 2012 - ¡Uno!, ¡Dos!, and ¡Tré! were released on September 21, November 9, and December 7 respectively and were met with generally positive reviews from critics, though fans where more lukewarm towards the albums.
  • 2013 - On April the band released ¡Cuatro!, a documentary about the making of the trilogy album.
  • 2014 - Demolicious, a compilation album that contains alternate versions and demos of songs from ¡Uno!, ¡Dos! and ¡Tré! recorded during the studio sessions of these albums, was released in April.
  • 2015 - Green Day performed its first concert in a year on April 16, the group played a set as Sweet Children with John Kiffmeyer, followed by a set as Green Day. On April 18, Green Day were inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame by Fall Out Boy.
  • 2016 - Green Day's album Revolution Radio was released on October 7.
  • 2017 - The band released its second greatest hits compilation, God's Favorite Band on November 17.
  • 2018 - In April The Longshot, Billie Joe Armstrong's new solo project, released the album Love is For Loosers and went on a mini-tour with members of the band Prima Donna and Jeff Matika (who also tours with Green Day).
